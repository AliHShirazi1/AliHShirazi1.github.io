About the Background

The dynamic animation you see is a 3D Mandelbulb fractal, rendered in real-time directly in your browser. It uses a technique called raymarching within a WebGL fragment shader. This allows for the generation of a complex, infinite 3D world without traditional polygons, calculated for every pixel on your screen.

Ali Heidari Shirazi

Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering

I am a Ph.D. candidate and Graduate Research Assistant at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, specializing in the dynamics of mechanical metamaterials. My work focuses on understanding how disorder and nonlinearities affect wave propagation characteristics.

My research involves using time-domain simulations, spacetime Fourier transforms, and C++ toolsets to analyze complex phenomena like chaos in bandgaps and wave diffraction. My goal is to resolve challenges in the homogenization of dispersive media to unlock new possibilities in vibration suppression and wave guiding.
